Self-Directed Classes

You know, I really regret taking Music Production class. I’ve realized throughout the course of 2 school years, that I do extremely poorly on classes that are self directed. Last year, I took Honors Robotics and Engineering. Now, the only reason I took the class was for the free A and honors credit (to boost GPA). I actually enjoyed the class a bit, since it was just basically a 90 minute free period to hanging with my friends. This year, however, I took music production class. My reasons for taking this class are different, it was because I wanted to be able to freely produce music, just like how I write code. I wanted to be able to think of an idea and just create it.

Honestly, I’m extremely disappointed at how the class is organized. That is – there’s pretty much no organization at all, the students in the class can essentially do whatever they want. It’s almost like there’s no point for the teacher to even be there for most of the time. It’s kind of depressing knowing that I’ve spent every B day 5th period for the past 7 months learning essentially nothing.

It really sucks I had high expectations for this class. I really wanted to be able to produce computerized songs and EDM beats, but that just doesn’t seem to be the case. Now, I’m really tired and bored of music class. Whenever I walk in the classroom, it feels like all of my previous desires to learn and become a musician instantly evaporates, leaving a lifeless corpse that is myself (*ahem* I’m so bad at writing ahh) (did that even make sense?). I just wanna say that I feel so unmotivated to do anything music related now.

Self-directed classes are stupid. There, I said it. It’s just really so, so stupid. Putting a group of impulse driven 14/15 year olds in a classroom, and giving them the freedom to do whatever they want to do won’t go well. What if they don’t know anything about the subject, and took the class wanting to have a teacher guide them through the subject like literally every other core class?

Like, literally, in the first day of class, the teacher was like okay, if you want to do an independent project, talk to me. Literally 80% of the class wanted to do an independent project, cause as teenagers, we’re lazy. It’s B5, the last period of class, what’s better than spending 90 minutes browsing the internet and looking at memes or texting friends? Unless you’re one of those rare ones who are mature and independent (not me), you’re most likely going to feel bored and unmotivated in a self-directed class.
